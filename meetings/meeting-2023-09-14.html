Consent & Permissions

See discussion thread on mailing list - https://lists.w3.org/Archives/Public/public-dpvcg/2023Sep/0000.html

harsh: The gist of this discussion is that we need terms based on the user' side of an agreement or reflecting the individual's perspective. In DPV, we model terms mostly on the basis of legal text - so these terms are 'fixed' or 'decisive'.

harsh: Whereas, modelling the perspectives of individuals would be an opinionated exercise, and thus be prone to not being satisfactory to all. Still, we can provide it as an additional extension, and additionally with different intended uses - such as for marketing, health, or specific applications.

iain: the internet has become more complex, and the current model is broken because it is only controlled by the entities at one end. We need to move to a different model. Similar to how the change is from oil based cars to electric cars is a better and greener change, we need something from the side of the individual.

harsh: And with the recent move towards decentralisation and federation of control, this is necessary to have the individual's perspectives be reflected. Although, in DPVCG, we should be careful and aim to not repeat efforts where existing ones exist and are usable. For example, IEEE P7012 is working on this.

iain: P7012 is not finalised and still figuring out where it is going. MyData has an ontology from the user's perspectives that is in development. The Finnish government has "individually controlled" (ic) as an ontology they are developing based on the MyData model as well.

harsh: as mentioned in the mailing list, we can start with ISO/IEC 29184 and do both legal and user vocabularies.
