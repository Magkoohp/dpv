Important Concepts for DPV

Jurisdictions - country, laws, DPAs, adequacy decisions

ISO standards - numbers, relation to terms, terminology of ISO, mapping to DPV concepts

risks - known risks and vocabulary of common risks e.g. data corruption, data breach

fundamental rights - (only eu) to provide relevance to impact assessments

All of the above are proposed as extensions / modules to the DPV rather than including them in core DPV

Process for concept proposal and resolution

For above and other concepts that are to be proposed as a group / collection, these shall be done through the mailing list.

The proposal shall have a review timeline within which interested members will comment / discuss.

The meeting calls will discuss the final resolution of such discussions, including a venue to resolve disagreements if any.

This is for ensuring DPV progresses sufficiently well for its goal to reach v1.0 by September.

Categories of Documentation

There are 5 different types of documentation we have for DPV

1. Primer for explaining what the DPV is, an overview of its concepts, some brief examples

2. Technical Specification - detailing what concepts exist

3. Use-Cases - that define situations the DPV should be capable for representing

4. Examples - showing how to use DPV, with some use-cases being expressed as examples, or examples coming from elsewhere

5. Guides - explanations on how to use DPV in some context, how to apply DPV towards an application

Template for Use-Cases

Jan is providing a shared Google Docs for discussion of use-case templates in which use-cases are described.

The requirement is for them to be simple enough to express and link with concepts.

Existing use-case template is comprehensive and needs to be minimised https://www.w3.org/community/dpvcg/wiki/Template_for_use-cases
